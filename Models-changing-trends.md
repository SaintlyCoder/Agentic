A clear trend is becoming apparent, especially with Anthropic's Opus models: subsequent versions require fewer tokens to perform predefined, well-prompted tasks, even as token expenses rise. While this enhanced efficiency is beneficial for executing explicit plans, it can, paradoxically, impede knowledge-intensive work. In such fields, the goal often involves interpretation, discovering new insights, and addressing unstated needs, areas where the rigid, rule-following design of models like Opus 4.7, which strictly adheres to explicit instructions, might prove restrictive.
Previously, with Opus 4.6, Anthropic provided "reasoning caps." Although many casual users and less experienced developers overlooked this feature, some discovered that the command line allowed for adjusting thinking capacity and limiting the tokens allocated for such processing. This functionality has now evolved with Opus 4.7, which introduces "adaptive thinking." This new approach can pose challenges for less experienced coders or those accustomed to a more intuitive, "vibe coding" style, making it difficult to achieve comparable results. The necessity stems from a demand for greater specificity; prompts must be more detailed and meticulously structured. Vague prompts lead Opus 4.7 to dedicate more processing time, whereas highly structured prompts allow it to process information more efficiently, requiring less "thinking" or token expenditure.
A notable shift observed from Opus 4.6 to Opus 4.7 is that the latter appears less inclined to spontaneously invoke sub-agents. The model tends to process tasks sequentially, rather than implicitly delegating to sub-agents, which can lead to context bloat. Anthropic, however, seems to be progressing towards a continuous, long prompt window incorporating compaction. One might assume this benefits them as a token-based company. Yet, it's also arguable that current "Max plans" aren't generating significant revenue and pose challenges for these platforms. This trend of unlimited plans may not be sustainable, especially considering recent pricing updates from GitHub and other providers. Consequently, the efficient utilization of our tokens has become paramount.
